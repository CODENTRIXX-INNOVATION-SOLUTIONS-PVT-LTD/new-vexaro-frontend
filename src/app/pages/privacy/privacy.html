Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Vexaro Courier Solutions Private Limited collects, uses, shares, stores, and protects personal data when distributors, merchants, authorised users, shipment recipients, and website visitors use Vexaro's website, dashboards, APIs, wallet tools, and shipment services.

1. Who We Are

  • Vexaro Courier Solutions Private Limited provides a logistics technology platform for merchant onboarding, shipment booking, tracking, wallet accounting, COD management, support, reports, and distributor operations.
  • Registered contact address: Flat 1113, 1st Floor, Chikitsak Nagar, Vijay Nagar, Indore, MP-452010.
  • Privacy contact: info@vexarocouriersolutions.com.

2. Data We Collect

Account and business data

  • Name, email, phone number, company name, address, role, account status, inviter relationship, and login timestamps.
  • Distributor and merchant hierarchy details, active or deleted status, notification preferences, and profile updates.
  • Warehouse information, including warehouse ID, contact person, address, pincode, city, state, country, phone, email, GST number, and carrier sync identifiers.
  • Warehouse address change requests, including current address, requested address, status, rejection reason, processor, and timestamps.

Shipment and recipient data

  • Pickup and delivery names, phone numbers, email addresses, addresses, landmarks, city, state, pincode, and country.
  • AWB numbers, merchant order references, invoice numbers, carrier AWBs, carrier names, labels, manifests, tracking URLs, status history, delivery attempts, delivery timestamps, and RTO or cancellation details.
  • Package and order details, including product name, SKU, quantity, selling price, discount, tax, declared value, declared weight, volumetric weight, billing weight, dimensions, service type, payment method, COD amount, and QC return data.
  • Saved address book records, labels, last-used timestamps, and soft-delete timestamps.

Wallet, payment, COD, and settlement data

  • Wallet balances, currency, active status, last recharge details, available balance, reserve information, and top-up policy status.
  • Ledger transactions, including type, amount, balance before and after, reference, shipment link, performer, notes, and timestamps.
  • Razorpay order and payment identifiers, payment status, payment method, bank, VPA, captured or failed timestamp, failure reason, refund status, and webhook event IDs.
  • Recharge requests, merchant recharge requests, refund requests, COD collection/remittance records, settlement records, review notes, and rejection reasons.

Support, dispute, security, and website data

  • Support ticket subject, description, category, priority, status, shipment references, replies, attachments, and resolution timestamps.
  • Dispute category, description, comments, attachments, assigned reviewer, resolution, resolved timestamp, weight-dispute proof images, extra charge, and expiry details.
  • Hashed passwords, hashed invite/reset/refresh tokens, hashed API keys, key preview, key permissions, expiry, last-used timestamp, and audit logs.
  • Contact form details, including name, email, phone, company, subject, and message.
  • Technical and security information such as IP address, request metadata, device/browser details, cookies or local storage required for authentication, and diagnostic logs where enabled by the platform or hosting environment.

3. How We Use Data

  • To create, verify, secure, activate, suspend, and manage user accounts and role-based access.
  • To onboard distributors and merchants, map merchant relationships, manage warehouses, and process warehouse change approvals.
  • To create shipments, generate AWBs, calculate charges, create labels, send orders to carrier partners, track shipments, manage returns, and process cancellations or RTO events.
  • To calculate pricing, billing weight, COD charges, margins, wallet debits, wallet credits, reserves, refunds, recharge requests, settlements, and COD remittance.
  • To provide dashboards, reports, exports, transaction history, shipment history, dispute history, support workflows, and notifications.
  • To detect fraud, prevent unauthorised access, enforce hierarchy rules, maintain audit logs, debug errors, and protect the platform.
  • To communicate service updates, support responses, shipment updates, payment updates, dispute updates, invites, password resets, and account notices.
  • To comply with legal, tax, accounting, regulatory, security, carrier, and dispute-resolution obligations.

4. How We Share Data

  • Carrier and logistics partners: shipment, sender, recipient, warehouse, package, COD, label, manifest, and tracking data required for pickup, transit, delivery, return, cancellation, and tracking.
  • Payment providers: payment, order, wallet top-up, refund, and verification data required to process payments through Razorpay, banks, UPI, card networks, or related providers.
  • Distributors and merchants: data visible within the authorised hierarchy, such as a distributor viewing its mapped merchants, shipments, wallets, recharge requests, COD records, reports, tickets, and disputes.
  • Service providers: hosting, database, email, logging, analytics, security, communication, and operational vendors who help run the platform.
  • Authorities and legal recipients: courts, regulators, tax authorities, law enforcement, auditors, or other recipients where required by law or needed to protect rights, safety, or property.
  • We do not sell personal data to advertisers. Recipient data is used for logistics, support, legal, and operational purposes, not unrelated advertising.

5. Payment and Credential Security

  • Vexaro does not store full card details. Card, UPI, bank, or payment instrument handling is performed by payment providers according to their own security controls.
  • Passwords are stored as hashes, not plain text.
  • Refresh tokens, invite tokens, reset tokens, and API keys are stored as hashes where applicable. Raw API keys are shown once at creation and are not stored in plain text.
  • Access is role-restricted so users can only access records allowed by their role and hierarchy.
  • Wallet ledger records are designed to be immutable after creation to support accounting and audit integrity.

6. Cookies and Local Storage

  • We use browser storage, tokens, and similar technologies for login sessions, authentication, security, preferences, dashboard operation, and local UI state.
  • The website or hosting environment may collect basic analytics or diagnostic data to understand performance, errors, traffic, and abuse prevention.
  • You can control cookies through your browser settings, but disabling required storage may prevent login or core platform features from working.

7. Data Retention

  • We retain account, shipment, wallet, transaction, COD, settlement, support, dispute, audit, and API records for as long as needed to provide services and satisfy operational, legal, tax, accounting, security, and dispute-resolution requirements.
  • Some records are soft-deleted or deactivated rather than immediately erased so past shipments, transactions, reports, audits, and legal records remain consistent.
  • Expired refresh tokens are configured for automatic cleanup after expiry plus a grace period. Other records may be retained longer where required by business or legal needs.
  • When data is no longer required, we may delete, anonymise, aggregate, or archive it according to our operational practices and applicable law.

8. Your Choices and Rights

  • You may request access to, correction of, or deletion of personal data where applicable under Indian law and where the request does not conflict with legal, accounting, tax, security, operational, or dispute obligations.
  • You can update certain profile, warehouse contact, notification, password, and API key settings through the dashboard where the feature is available for your role.
  • You can opt out of non-essential communications where supported. Service, security, payment, shipment, support, and legal notices may still be sent.
  • Merchants are responsible for ensuring they have a lawful basis to provide recipient and customer data to Vexaro for shipment delivery and related services.
  • To exercise privacy rights or ask questions, contact info@vexarocouriersolutions.com.

9. Recipient and Customer Data

  • Recipient data is usually provided by merchants or distributors when booking shipments or saving address book entries.
  • We use recipient data to create shipments, arrange pickup and delivery, generate labels, process returns, provide tracking, handle COD, resolve support issues, and meet carrier or legal obligations.
  • Merchants should not upload recipient data unless it is accurate, relevant, and authorised for logistics processing.

10. Children's Data

  • The Vexaro platform is intended for business use by authorised adults and business representatives.
  • We do not knowingly create platform accounts for children. If personal data about a child appears in shipment recipient details, it should be limited to what is necessary for delivery and related services.

11. International Transfers

  • Vexaro primarily operates for Indian logistics workflows, but cloud, carrier, payment, communication, analytics, or support providers may process data in locations allowed by their infrastructure and applicable law.
  • Where required, we rely on contractual, technical, and organisational measures to protect transferred data.

12. Changes to This Policy

  • We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in law, product features, carriers, payments, security practices, or business operations.
  • The updated policy will be posted on this page with a revised last-updated date. Continued use of the platform after an update means the updated policy applies.
